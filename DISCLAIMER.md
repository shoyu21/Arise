# Clinical Disclaimer

**ARISE - Awakening & Respiratory Integration for Safe Extubation**

Last Updated: December 2024

---

## Important Medical Disclaimer

### NOT A SUBSTITUTE FOR CLINICAL JUDGMENT

**ARISE is a clinical decision support tool only. It does NOT replace the expertise, judgment, and responsibility of qualified healthcare professionals.**

---

## Purpose of ARISE

ARISE is designed to:

- ✅ Assist healthcare professionals in systematic assessment
- ✅ Provide evidence-based reference information
- ✅ Support clinical decision-making processes
- ✅ Facilitate ICU liberation protocol implementation
- ✅ Synthesize published clinical evidence

---

## Limitations

ARISE does NOT:

- ❌ Provide medical diagnoses
- ❌ Prescribe treatments or interventions
- ❌ Account for all patient-specific factors
- ❌ Replace institutional protocols or policies
- ❌ Guarantee clinical outcomes
- ❌ Substitute for direct patient assessment

---

## Clinical Decision Responsibility

### Healthcare Professional Responsibility

All clinical decisions must be made by qualified healthcare professionals who:

1. Have direct knowledge of the patient's complete clinical picture
2. Can perform physical examination and assessment
3. Have access to complete medical records
4. Can integrate information ARISE does not capture
5. Apply professional judgment to each unique situation
6. Accept full responsibility for patient care decisions

### Factors ARISE Cannot Assess

ARISE cannot evaluate:

- Visual patient assessment findings
- Subjective patient symptoms and complaints
- Real-time changes in patient condition
- Institutional-specific protocols and resources
- Staffing and monitoring capabilities
- Patient and family preferences
- Contraindications not captured in the tool
- Nuanced clinical circumstances

---

## Evidence Limitations

### General Evidence Limitations

The evidence synthesized in ARISE:

- Is based on published research literature
- May have publication bias toward positive findings
- Derives from specific study populations that may differ from your patients
- May not reflect the most recent research
- Should be interpreted in clinical context

### Threshold Values

Clinical thresholds in ARISE:

- Are derived from research populations
- May not apply to all patient populations
- Should be viewed as guides, not absolute cutoffs
- May require institutional adaptation
- Do not account for individual patient variation

### Special Populations

Evidence for special populations (COPD, Heart Failure, Obesity, Elderly) may:

- Be based on limited studies
- Have heterogeneous definitions
- Require additional clinical consideration
- Not capture all relevant subgroups
